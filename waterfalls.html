Magnificent Waterfalls

Experience nature’s power and beauty in India’s most breathtaking waterfalls.

Famous Waterfalls in India

Jog Falls, Karnataka

One of India’s highest waterfalls, formed by the Sharavathi River plunging from 830 feet. Best visited during the monsoon.

Nearby: Sharavathi Valley, Sigandur Temple

Athirappilly Falls, Kerala

Known as the “Niagara of India”, surrounded by lush Sholayar forests. A popular spot for film shoots and nature lovers.

Nearby: Vazhachal Falls, Sholayar Dam

Dhuandhar Falls, Madhya Pradesh

Located in Bhedaghat near Jabalpur, this mighty fall creates a misty “smoke-like” effect — hence the name Dhuandhar.

Nearby: Marble Rocks, Narmada River boating

Hogenakkal Falls, Tamil Nadu

Called the “Niagara of South India”, famous for its coracle rides and carbonated rocks.

Chitrakote Falls, Chhattisgarh

The widest waterfall in India, often compared to Niagara Falls for its grandeur.

Nohkalikai Falls, Meghalaya

India’s tallest plunge waterfall, cascading from emerald cliffs near Cherrapunji.

Hebbe Falls, Karnataka

Hidden amidst coffee plantations of Chikmagalur, offering a serene and misty retreat.

Kempty Falls, Uttarakhand

A popular hill-station waterfall near Mussoorie, perfect for picnics and cool dips.
